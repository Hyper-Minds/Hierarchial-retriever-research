## COURT NAME
Supreme Court of India

## PETITIONER NAME(S)
Vinay Aggarwal

## RESPONDENT NAME(S)
The State of Haryana and Ors.

## CASE BACKGROUND
The case revolves around an FIR No.215/2022 registered under ss.120B, 177, 406, 420, 467, 468, 471, 506 of IPC against the petitioner, Vinay Aggarwal, who was made an accused in the FIR. The complainant filed a petition under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC) seeking transfer of investigation from the civil police of Haryana to the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI).

## PETITIONER ARGUMENTS
The petitioner argued that the High Court's order directing CBI investigation was not sustainable as the allegations made by the complainant were vague and unsubstantiated.

## RESPONDENT ARGUMENT
The respondent argued that the High Court had the power to direct CBI investigation under Section 482 CrPC, but it should be exercised sparingly and only in exceptional cases where there is a need for credibility and confidence in investigations.

## ISSUES DISCUSSED IN COURT
* Sustainability of the High Court's order directing CBI investigation.
* Whether the allegations made by the complainant are sufficient to hand over the case to CBI.
* The power of the High Court to direct CBI investigation under Section 482 CrPC.

## FINAL DECISION
The Supreme Court allowed the appeal and set aside the High Court's order directing CBI investigation, holding that the allegations made by the complainant were not substantiated and the case did not meet the criteria for CBI investigation.

## COURT'S REASONING
The court cited precedents such as State of W.B. v. Committee for Protection of Democratic Rights (2010) 3 SCC 571, which held that Constitutional Courts are fully empowered to direct CBI investigation, but it should be exercised sparingly and only in exceptional cases where there is a need for credibility and confidence in investigations.
