## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): M/s Naresh Potteries

## Respondent Name(s): M/s Aarti Industries and Another

## Case Background: 
A complaint was filed by Sh. Neeraj Kumar on behalf of the appellant-firm, M/s Naresh Potteries, against Respondent No.1, under Section 138 of the NI Act.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The petitioner argued that the High Court erred in quashing the summoning order issued to Respondent No.1, as there was no specific averment regarding Sh. Neeraj Kumar's knowledge about the transaction in the relevant documents.

## Respondent Argument:
The respondent argued that the power of attorney holder did not have personal knowledge of the facts giving rise to the criminal proceedings, and therefore, the complaint was defective.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed the guidelines laid down in A.C. Narayanan v. State of Maharashtra (2014) 11 SCC 790, and whether the averments made by Sh. Neeraj Kumar were sufficient to establish his knowledge and authorisation.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court allowed the appeal, quashed the High Court's judgment and order, and restored the complaint to the file of the Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Khurja, Bulandshahar to be heard and decided on its own merits.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court held that Sh. Neeraj Kumar had categorically averred his knowledge and authorisation in the documents, making it clear that he was well-equipped and duly authorised to initiate criminal proceedings against Respondent No.1.
