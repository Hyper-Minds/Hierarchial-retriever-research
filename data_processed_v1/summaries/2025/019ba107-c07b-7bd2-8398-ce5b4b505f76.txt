## Court Name: Supreme Court of India
## Petitioner Name(s): The Superintending Engineer, Operation, Telangana State Southern Power Distribution Company Ltd. & Ors.
## Respondent Name(s): Ch. Bhaskara Chary
## Case Background: The case revolves around the appointment of Lower Division Clerk (LDC) to the respondent, Ch. Bhaskara Chary, despite being placed higher in the seniority list. The High Court directed the appellant to consider the respondent's appointment to the post of LDC or any other suitable or supernumerary post.
## Petitioner Arguments: The petitioner argued that the list relied on by the High Court is not a seniority list but only a list of candidates having minimum qualification, who are eligible to attend the interview.
## Respondent Argument: The respondent argued that he must be treated at par with other appointed candidates as he is higher in the list of eligible candidates and has relatively lesser man-days than them.
## Issues discussed in Court: The court discussed whether the respondent's appointment should be considered by the appellant on par with other appointed candidates, despite being placed higher in the seniority list.
## Final Decision: The Supreme Court directed the appellant to reconsider the respondent's case for appointment to the post of LDC or any other equivalent post in which a vacancy may exist, and pass appropriate orders within 6 weeks from the date of the judgment.
## Court's Reasoning: The court reasoned that since candidates with lesser man-days than the respondent were appointed pursuant to the High Court's direction in certain other writ petitions, the respondent must be treated at par with them as he is relatively better positioned in the list of eligible candidates.
