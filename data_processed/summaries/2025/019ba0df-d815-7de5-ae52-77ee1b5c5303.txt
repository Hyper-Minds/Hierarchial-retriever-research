## Court Name:
Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s):
Bernard Francis Joseph Vaz and Others

## Respondent Name(s):
Government of Karnataka and Others

## Case Background:
The case revolves around the acquisition of land by the Government of Karnataka for a project, which led to the displacement of the appellants. The appellants claimed that they were not paid adequate compensation for their land, which was acquired under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The appellants argued that they were deprived of their property without being paid adequate compensation in accordance with law. They also claimed that the delay in payment of compensation and the non-payment of the same for over 22 years is a breach of Article 300-A of the Constitution.

## Respondent Argument:
The respondents argued that the delay in payment of compensation was not attributable to them but was on account of inaction on the part of the State. They also claimed that the appellants had no right to challenge the award passed by the SLAO.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed the issues of adequate compensation, delay in payment, and the constitutional right to property under Article 300-A of the Constitution.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court quashed the judgment and order passed by the Division Bench of the High Court and allowed the writ petition filed by the appellants. The court directed the SLAO to pass a fresh award taking into account the market value prevailing as on 22nd April 2019 within two months from the date of this judgment.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court relied on various precedents, including Vidya Devi and Others v. State of Haryana, Union of India v. Mahendra Girji, and Mansaram v. S.P. Pathak, to hold that the delay in payment of compensation is a breach of Article 300-A of the Constitution.

- Mansaram v. S.P. Pathak
