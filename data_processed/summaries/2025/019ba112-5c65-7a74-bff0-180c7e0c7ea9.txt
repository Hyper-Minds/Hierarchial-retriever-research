## COURT NAME:
Supreme Court of India

## PETITIONER NAME(S):
Shri. Masaidevi Vividh Karyakari Sahakari Seva Sanstha 
Maryadit Warewadi 

## RESPONDENT NAME(S):
The State of Maharashtra & Ors.

## CASE BACKGROUND: 
The case revolves around the registration of a primary agricultural credit cooperative society (PACCS) in a revenue village. The petitioner, Shri. Masaidevi Vividh Karyakari Sahakari Seva Sanstha Maryadit Warewadi, is seeking registration as a PACCS. However, the State of Maharashtra & Ors. has rejected its application due to lack of economic viability.

## PETITIONER ARGUMENTS:
The petitioner argues that it is practically not possible for any society to have share capital of Rs. 5 lakhs at the time of applying for registration. The petitioner also submits that it has made a genuine effort to fulfill all the required documents at the time of filing the application for registration.

## RESPONDENT ARGUMENT:
The respondent argues that the eligibility for registration cannot be isolated from the impact on an existing society, if any. The respondent also submits that the appellant has failed to satisfy the threshold criteria and conditions to establish its own viability.

## ISSUES DISCUSSED IN COURT:
* Economic viability of the society
* Registration of PACCS
* Impact on existing societies

## FINAL DECISION:
The Supreme Court of India has dismissed the appeals, upholding the order passed by the High Court of Judicature at Bombay. The court held that the State had ignored the basic criteria for registration and allowed the registration of the society without fulfilling the necessary pre-requisites.

## COURT'S REASONING:
The court reasoned that the constitution of the Committee and the examination of the proposal by the Committee is a vital part of the Policy Directives of the State Government. The court also held that the State's decision to relax the financial viability criteria was not in accordance with its own guidelines.
