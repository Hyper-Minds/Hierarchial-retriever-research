## Court Name: High Court of Judicature at Madras

## Petitioner Name(s): Chief Revenue Controlling Officer Cum Inspector General of Registration, & Ors.

## Respondent Name(s): P. Babu

## Case Background: The case involves a dispute over the market value of two properties, which were registered as documents under the Indian Stamp Act, 1899. The petitioner, Chief Revenue Controlling Officer Cum Inspector General of Registration, & Ors., has challenged the determination of the market value of the properties by the Collector (Stamps).

## Petitioner Arguments: The petitioner argues that the Registering Officer did not provide any reasons for concluding that the sale consideration shown in the sale deeds was undervalued.

## Respondent Argument: The respondent, P. Babu, has filed objections against the determination of the market value of the properties by the Collector (Stamps).

## Issues discussed in Court: The court discussed the issues of whether the Registering Officer had the power to refer the matter to the Collector for determining the market value of the properties, and whether the Collector's decision was correct.

## Final Decision: The High Court dismissed the appeals filed by the petitioner.

## Court's Reasoning: The court held that the Registering Officer did not provide any reasons for concluding that the sale consideration shown in the sale deeds was undervalued, and therefore, the Collector's decision was not justified. The court also held that the Collector had the power to determine the market value of the properties under Section 47-A of the Indian Stamp Act, 1899.
