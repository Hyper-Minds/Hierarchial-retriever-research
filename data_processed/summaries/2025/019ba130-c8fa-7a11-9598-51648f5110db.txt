## Court Name: Supreme Court of India
## Petitioner Name(s): Shabeen Ahmad 
## Respondent Name(s): The State of Uttar Pradesh & Anr. 

## Case Background: 
The case pertains to the correctness of the order passed by the High Court granting bail to in-laws of the deceased wife, charged for causing dowry death of the deceased.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The Appellant (Shabeen Ahmad) has challenged the orders granting bail to the Respondent-accused, arguing that the High Court erred in overlooking substantial material indicating their involvement in the alleged offence.

## Respondent Argument: 
The Respondents (Accused Nos. 2, 3, 4, and 5) have argued that they should be granted bail as they have no prior criminal history, some of them are women, and certain co-accused had already been granted bail.

## Issues discussed in Court:
Stricter judicial scrutiny necessary in matters where a young woman loses her life in her matrimonial home under highly suspicious circumstances so soon after marriage, particularly where the record points to persistent harassment over unmet dowry demands.

## Final Decision: 
The bail granted to Accused No.2 (father-in-law) and Accused No.3 (mother-in-law) is hereby cancelled. The bail granted to Accused No.4 (Saba) and Accused No.5 (Ayasha) is upheld.

## Court's Reasoning:
The Court found that the Respondent-accused did not deserve continued protection of bail as the evidence indicated their direct involvement in the fatal events. However, leniency was shown towards Accused No.4 and Accused No.5 due to their personal and educational circumstances.
