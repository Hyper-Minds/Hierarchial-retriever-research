## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): Godrej Projects Development Limited

## Respondent Name(s): Anil Karlekar & Ors.

## Case Background: The petitioner, Godrej Projects Development Limited, is a developer who had entered into an agreement with the respondents, Anil Karlekar and others, for sale of apartments. The respondents had paid Rs. 51,12,310 as earnest money deposit (EMD). However, they sought cancellation of the allotment due to a sharp decline in prices, claiming that they could buy another property at a lower rate.

## Petitioner Arguments: The petitioner argued that the respondents had failed to fulfill the conditions stipulated in the agreement and were not entitled to cancel the allotment. They also claimed that the respondents had sought cancellation after the possession was offered to them.

## Respondent Argument: The respondents argued that they had the right to cancel the allotment due to the sharp decline in prices, which made it difficult for them to purchase another property at a lower rate.

## Issues discussed in Court: The court discussed whether the respondents were entitled to cancel the allotment and whether the petitioner was liable to pay compensation to the respondents.

## Final Decision: The Supreme Court allowed the appeal of the petitioner and directed that the amount paid by the respondents be refunded, minus 10% of the base selling price (BSP), which is a reasonable amount for forfeiture as earnest money.

## Court's Reasoning: The court relied on its previous judgments in cases such as Pioneer Urban Land and Infrastructure Limited v. Anil Karlekar & Ors., Wing Commander Arifur Rahman Khan and Aleya Sultana and others v. DLF Southern Homes Private Limited (Now Known as Begur OMR Homes Private Limited) and Ireo Grace Realtech Private Limited, which held that one-sided agreements between developers and apartment purchasers are unfair trade practices.

## List of Important Precedents Cited: Pioneer Urban Land and Infrastructure Limited v. Anil Karlekar & Ors., Wing Commander Arifur Rahman Khan and Aleya Sultana and others v. DLF Southern Homes Private Limited (Now Known as Begur OMR Homes Private Limited), Ireo Grace Realtech Private Limited
