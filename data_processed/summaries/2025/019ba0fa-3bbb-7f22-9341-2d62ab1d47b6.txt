## COURT NAME: 
Supreme Court of India

## PETITIONER NAME(S): 
Sanjay Dutt & Ors.

## RESPONDENT NAME(S): 
The State of Haryana & Anr.

## CASE BACKGROUND: 
According to the Range Forest Officer, the appellants (director and office bearers of the company) had illegally uprooted trees with JCB, destroyed them, and violated the section 4 of the Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900. The question that arises for consideration is whether vicarious liability that can be attached to any of the directors or any office bearers of the company.

## PETITIONER ARGUMENTS: 
The petitioners argue that there is no vicarious liability that can be attached to any of the directors or any office bearers of the company. They claim that it is the individual liability or the act that would make the person concerned liable for being prosecuted for the offence punishable under Section 19 of the Act, 1900.

## RESPONDENT ARGUMENT: 
The respondent argues that the appellants are responsible for the alleged offence and should be held liable. However, the court finds that there are no allegations worth the name in the complaint that the three appellants are directly responsible for uprooting the trees with the aid of Bulldozers or JCB machines or causing damage to the environment.

## ISSUES DISCUSSED IN COURT: 
The court discusses whether vicarious liability can be attached to any of the directors or any office bearers of the company, and if so, under what circumstances.

## FINAL DECISION: 
The Supreme Court allows the appeal and quashes the complaint and order taking cognizance of the said complaint.

## COURT'S REASONING: 
The court holds that there is no vicarious liability that can be attached to any of the directors or any office bearers of the company, as it is not specifically provided in the statute. The court also finds that the allegations against the appellants are absolutely vague and do not make out a case for putting them to trial.
