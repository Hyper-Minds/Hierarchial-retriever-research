## Court Name: Supreme Court of India
## Petitioner Name(s): Rajnish Singh @ Soni
## Respondent Name(s): State of U.P. and Another

## Case Background: 
The case pertains to the liability of the appellant to be prosecuted for committing rape upon the complainant by giving her a false promise of marriage; and the correctness of the order passed by the High Court dismissing the petition seeking the quashing of the criminal proceedings arising out of the chargesheet u/ss.376, 384, 323, 504, 506 IPC.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The appellant argued that the entire case as set out in the impugned FIR and the chargesheet is false and cooked up. The complainant is a major educated girl, who was fully conscious of the consequences of the intimate relationship which flourished between her and the appellant for a period of almost 16 years. The acts of repeated intimacy and sexual relations were totally consensual in nature and were not established under any false promise, threat, duress or coercion.

## Respondent Argument:
The State and learned counsel appearing for the complainant opposed the submissions advanced by the counsel for the appellant. They urged that the appellant won over the confidence of the complainant by giving her false assurances of marriage and based on such promise he sexually exploited her, when in fact, he had no intentions to marry her.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed whether the appellant is liable for the offence of rape if the victim has wilfully agreed to maintain sexual relations. The court also considered whether the allegations made by the complainant are full of material contradictions and are ex facie unbelievable.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court allowed the appeal, quashed the order dated 24th April, 2023 passed by the High Court of Judicature at Allahabad, and set aside all the consequent proceedings sought to be taken thereunder against the appellant.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court held that the complainant’s allegations seem to be well-arranged story. The complainant, being a highly qualified major woman continued unabatedly in a consensual intimate sexual relationship with the appellant over a period of 16 years, which is sufficient to conclude that there was never an element of force or deceit in the relationship. The court also held that the allegations of the complainant are full of material contradictions and are ex facie unbelievable.
