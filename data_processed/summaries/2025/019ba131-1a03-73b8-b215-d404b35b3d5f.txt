## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): Sharmila Velamur

## Respondent Name(s): V. Sanjay and Ors.

## Case Background: 
Aadith Ramadorai, an 8 to 10-year-old child with cognitive limitations, was taken by his father, the Appellant, from India to the US for schooling. However, upon arrival in the US, Aadith's father passed away, leaving him with his younger brother, Arjun, who also has intellectual disabilities and autism spectrum disorder. The Respondent, V. Sanjay, is Aadith's mother, who claims that she was not given adequate notice of her son's departure from India and seeks to have him returned to India for his "best interests."

## Petitioner Arguments: 
The Appellant argues that Aadith cannot make independent decisions due to his cognitive limitations and that it is in his best interests to continue residing with his brother in the US, where he can complete his schooling and receive specialized care.

## Respondent Argument: 
The Respondent argues that Aadith's wishes should be the sole criterion for determining his place of residence, citing cases such as Girish v. Radhamony K. and Suchita Srivastava v. Chandigarh Admn.

## Issues discussed in Court:
- Aadith's capacity to make independent decisions
- Aadith's best interests and welfare
- The principle of "best interests of the child"
- The role of Foreign Courts in determining custody

## Final Decision: 
The Supreme Court allows the appeal and sets aside the Impugned Judgment of the High Court, directing that Aadith Ramadorai be returned to the US with his brother Arjun under the guardianship of the Appellant.

## Court's Reasoning:
The Court holds that Aadith is incapable of making independent decisions due to his cognitive limitations and that it is in his best interests to continue residing with his brother in the US. The Court also emphasizes the importance of considering the "best interests of the child" principle, which takes precedence over Foreign Courts' orders.
