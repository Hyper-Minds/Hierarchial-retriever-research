## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): Sri Mahesh v. Sangram & Ors.

## Respondent Name(s): None specified

## Case Background: The case involves a dispute over the ownership and distribution of properties among heirs after the death of defendant No.1, who adopted the appellant/plaintiff as her son.

## Petitioner Arguments: The appellant/plaintiff challenged the validity of several alienations made by defendant No.1, including sales and gifts to other parties, arguing that they were not authorized or were made without his consent.

## Respondent Argument: The respondent argued that the alienations were valid and binding on the appellant/plaintiff, citing the doctrine of relation back and the provisions of Section 14(1) of the Act and Section 12(c) of the Act of 1956.

## Issues discussed in Court: The court discussed the validity of several alienations made by defendant No.1, including sales and gifts to other parties, and whether they were authorized or made without the consent of the appellant/plaintiff.

## Final Decision: The appeal is partly allowed. The concurrent finding of the courts below that the sale deed dated 13.12.2007 in favour of defendant Nos.2 and 3 is valid and that the appellant/plaintiff is not entitled to any share in ‘A’ schedule property is confirmed. The appeal against the judgment in RFA No.100168 of 2018, which reversed the trial Court's finding that the gift deed dated 27.08.2008 was null and void, is allowed.

## Court's Reasoning: The court relied on the doctrine of relation back and the provisions of Section 14(1) of the Act and Section 12(c) of the Act of 1956 to hold that all lawful alienations made by defendant No.1 would bind the appellant/plaintiff. The court also cited several precedents, including Sripad Gajanan Suthankar's case and Kasabai Tukaram Karvar's case.

## List of Important Precedents Cited: Sripad Gajanan Suthankar's case, Kasabai Tukaram Karvar's case
