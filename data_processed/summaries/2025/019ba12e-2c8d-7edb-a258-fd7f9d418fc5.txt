## Court Name:
Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s):
Gulshan Kumar

## Respondent Name(s):
Institute of Banking Personnel Selection & Ors.

## Case Background:
The petitioner, Gulshan Kumar, filed a writ petition against the Institute of Banking Personnel Selection & Ors., alleging that they had not been providing reasonable accommodations to persons with disabilities (PwD) candidates in their examinations. The petitioner claimed that the Respondent No. 5's Office Memorandum dated 10.08.2022 failed to extend benefits to PwD candidates, despite the directions of the Supreme Court.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The petitioner argued that the Office Memorandum did not provide for facilities other than a scribe and compensatory time, which was contrary to the provisions of the RPwD Act, 2016. The petitioner also claimed that the absence of a clear grievance redressal mechanism caused inconvenience and injustice to PwD candidates.

## Respondent Argument:
The respondents stated that they had been following the directions of the Supreme Court and the guidelines issued by Respondent No. 5 in the Office Memorandum dated 10.08.2022.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed the issues of reasonable accommodation, discrimination against PwD candidates, and the need for a uniform memorandum for examinations applicable to all PwD candidates.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court directed the Respondent No. 5 to revisit the Office Memorandum dated 10.08.2022, remove the restrictions, and grant relaxations in a reasonable manner. The court also ordered the Respondent No. 5 to ensure strict compliance with the guidelines issued by the Supreme Court.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court reasoned that the rights under Articles 19 and 21 of the Constitution can be enforced even against private entities and that the benefits conferred by the statute should be provided for all PwD candidates.
