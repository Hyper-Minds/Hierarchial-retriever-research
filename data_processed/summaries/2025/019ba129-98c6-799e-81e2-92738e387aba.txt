## Court Name:
Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s):
Kiran

## Respondent Name(s):
Rajkumar Jivraj Jain & Anr.

## Case Background:
The case revolves around an incident where the petitioner, Kiran, was allegedly insulted with a casteist slur by the respondent, Rajkumar Jivraj Jain. The incident took place outside Kiran's house in public view, and he was also threatened to be burned alive. The FIR lodged by Kiran alleged that he was addressed with abusive casteist utterance "Mangatyano" by the accused.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The petitioner argued that the respondent committed an offence under Section 3(1)(r) of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989. The petitioner claimed that the use of the word "Mangatyano" was with a clear intention to humiliate him because he belonged to the said Scheduled Caste community.

## Respondent Argument:
The respondent argued that no offence was made out from the bare reading of the FIR. The High Court had granted anticipatory bail to the respondent, which was challenged by Kiran in this appeal.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed the applicability of Section 18 of the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, which excludes the applicability of Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The court also considered whether the incident took place in a public view.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court set aside the order of the High Court and allowed the appeal. The anticipatory bail granted to the respondent was cancelled.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court held that the offence under Section 3(1)(r) of the SC/ST Act was made out from the bare reading of the FIR. The court also considered the fact that the incident took place outside Kiran's house in public view, which is a place within public view.
