## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): Ramesh Chand (D) Thr. Lrs.

## Respondent Name(s): Suresh Chand and Anr.

## Case Background: The case revolves around a dispute over the ownership of a suit property, which was originally owned by Shri Kundan Lal, the father of the plaintiff and defendant No. 1. After Kundan Lal's demise, the succession opened up, and the will dated 16.05.1996 propounded by the plaintiff is being challenged.

## Petitioner Arguments: The petitioner argues that the will is valid and that the respondent is not entitled to any share in the suit property.

## Respondent Argument: The respondent argues that the will is not valid due to suspicious circumstances surrounding its execution, including the fact that Kundan Lal had four children, including the plaintiff and defendant No. 1, and that there is no evidence of estrangement between the father and his children.

## Issues discussed in Court: The court discussed the validity of the will, the doctrine of part-performance under Section 53A of the TP Act, and the right to possession of the suit property.

## Final Decision: The Supreme Court sets aside the impugned judgment and allows the appeal. The suit of the plaintiff is dismissed subject to certain observations.

## Court's Reasoning: The court held that the will was not proved in accordance with law, as required by Section 63 of the Succession Act, 1925 and Section 68 of the Evidence Act, 1872. Additionally, the court found that the suspicious circumstances surrounding the execution of the will could not be removed by the plaintiff.

## List of Important Precedents Cited: H. Venkatachala Iyengar v. B.N. Thimmajamma and Others, Meena Pradhan and Others v. Kamla Pradhan and Another

## List of Acts: Indian Succession Act, 1925; Evidence Act, 1872; Transfer of Property Act, 1881
