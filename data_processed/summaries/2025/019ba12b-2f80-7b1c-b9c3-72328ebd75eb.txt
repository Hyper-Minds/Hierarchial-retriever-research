## Court Name: Supreme Court of India

## Petitioner Name(s): Kalluri Naga Narasimha Abhiram & Ors. Etc.

## Respondent Name(s): The State of Telangana & Ors. Etc.

## Case Background: 
The case pertains to the validity of the amended Rules of 2017, which provide for reservation in admissions to medical colleges in the State of Telangana. The petitioners, who did not fall under the definition of "local candidates," challenged the rule on the grounds that it was exclusionary and violative of Article 14 of the Constitution.

## Petitioner Arguments: 
The petitioners argued that the amended rule was arbitrary and unconstitutional as it excluded students who studied outside Telangana but had permanent residence or domicile in the State. They also claimed that the rule created an unintelligible differentia between students based on their acquiring qualifications from schools within the Municipal limits or AUDA.

## Respondent Argument: 
The State of Telangana argued that the amended rule was permissible as it provided for a "Competent Authority Quota" and traced its power to the Regulation Act of 1983, which had been brought out under Entry 25 of List III, Seventh Schedule read with Article 371D and the Presidential Order of 1974.

## Issues discussed in Court: 
The court discussed the validity of the amended rule, whether it was exclusionary, arbitrary, and violative of Article 14. The court also considered the principle of reading down and whether the amended rule had to be struck down or read down.

## Final Decision: 
The Supreme Court upheld the Rules of 2017 as amended in 2024, with a proviso to Rule 3 that would allay the grievances of students who were taken out of the State by compulsion of their parents' employment. The court allowed the appeals of the State and the University.

## Court's Reasoning: 
The court relied on previous decisions such as Pradeep Jain v. Union of India, Anand Madaan v. State of Haryana, and Rajdeep Ghosh v. State of Assam, which had laid down that the requirement of residence/domicile for admission to MBBS/BDS courses was permissible.

- Pradeep Jain v. Union of India
- Anand Madaan v. State of Haryana
- Rajdeep Ghosh v. State of Assam
