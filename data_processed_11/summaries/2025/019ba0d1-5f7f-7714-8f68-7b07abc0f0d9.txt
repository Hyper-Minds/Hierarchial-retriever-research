## Court Name : Supreme Court of India
## Petitioner Name(s) : Gopal Krishan & Ors.
## Respondent Name(s) : Daulat Ram & Ors.

## Case Background: 
The case revolves around the validity of a Will executed by Sanjhi Ram, who owned 1/4th share of a land. The testator had no children and resided with his nephew Gopal Krishan. He executed a Will on 7th November 2005 and passed away the next day on 8th November 2005.

## Petitioner Arguments: 
The petitioners argued that the Will was forged and fabricated, and the consequent sale deeds cannot be held invalid.

## Respondent Argument: 
The respondents argued that the attesting witness did not state that the act of affixing his thumb impression on the Will was at the direction of the Testator, thereby not meeting the requirement stipulated under Section 63 of the Succession Act, 1925.

## Issues discussed in Court: 
* The meaning of the phrase 'direction of the testator' under Section 63(c) of the Succession Act, 1925.
* Whether the attesting witness's testimony met the requirements of Section 63(c).

## Final Decision: 
The Supreme Court allowed the appeal and set aside the judgment of the High Court. The court held that the attesting witness's testimony did meet the requirements of Section 63(c), as he had seen the deceased affix his mark on the Will.

## Court's Reasoning: 
The court relied on the language of Section 63(c) of the Act, which uses the word 'OR'. It stated that each Will shall be attested by two or more witnesses who have seen the Testator sign or affix his mark on the Will OR has seen some other persons sign the Will in the presence and by the direction of the Testator.
