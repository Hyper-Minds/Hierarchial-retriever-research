## Court Name: Supreme Court of India
## Petitioner Name(s): Jomon K.K.
## Respondent Name(s): Shajimon P. & Ors. Etc.

## Case Background:
The petitioner, Jomon K.K., had applied for the post of Lascar in the State Public Service Commission (KPSC) but was not selected due to his lack of a current Lascar's licence. The petitioner argued that he should have been considered eligible since he possessed a Syrang's licence.

## Petitioner Arguments:
The petitioner argued that the non-joinder of necessary parties, specifically those holding Syrang's licences, in the proceedings before the Tribunal was a significant issue. He also argued that the advertisement for the post did not require any qualifications other than what was statutorily laid down by the Special Rules.

## Respondent Argument:
The respondent argued that the petitioner had not been included as a necessary party in the proceedings due to non-joinder, which meant that his rights were not affected by the Tribunal's order. The respondent also argued that the advertisement required a current Lascar's licence, and the petitioner did not possess one.

## Issues discussed in Court:
The court discussed issues related to non-joinder of necessary parties, statutory qualifications, and the nature of duties required for the post of Lascar.

## Final Decision:
The Supreme Court dismissed the appeals, holding that the petitioner had gained entry through an illegal process and that the court's role was limited to being dispute-settlers. The court did not exercise its power under Article 142 of the Constitution to ignore the illegality and invalidity of the appointment.

## Court's Reasoning:
The court relied on several precedents, including Ranjan Kumar v. State of Bihar, Kulwant Singh v. Dayaram, and M. Tripura Sundari Devi v. Union of India, to establish that selectees who are appointed or promoted must be arrayed as parties in the original proceedings where their selections are challenged.

## List of Important Precedents Cited:
- Ranjan Kumar v. State of Bihar
- Kulwant Singh v. Dayaram
- M. Tripura Sundari Devi v. Union of India
