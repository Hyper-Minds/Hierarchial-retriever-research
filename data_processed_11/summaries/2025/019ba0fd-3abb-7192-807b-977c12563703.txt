## COURT NAME : Supreme Court of India
## PETITIONER NAME(s) : Maukam Singh & Others 
## RESPONDENT NAME(s) : State of Madhya Pradesh

## CASE BACKGROUND: 
The case revolves around the conviction and sentencing of six accused persons for murder and injuries sustained by three grandchildren of a deceased person. The accused were charged under Sections 302, 323, and 324 read with Section 34 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) for their alleged role in the altercation that led to the death.

## PETITIONER ARGUMENTS: 
The petitioners argued that there was no premeditation or intention to cause death; not even a remotest knowledge of an injury being caused. They also claimed that the ocular witnesses' testimony indicated that the accused carried cutting weapons, but the injury on the deceased could have been caused by an accidental fall.

## RESPONDENT ARGUMENT: 
The respondent argued that the injured witnesses spoke in tandem and it was the accused who trespassed into the house of the victims. They claimed that there was absolutely no provocation on the part of the victims and that the accused came to the house with deadly weapons, clearly with the intention to harm the inmates.

## ISSUES DISCUSSED IN COURT: 
The court discussed whether the order passed by the High Court upholding the conviction and sentence imposed on the accused was justified. They also examined the testimony of witnesses with a hawk's eye to understand whether it was truthful or if the witnesses were to be disbelieved.

## FINAL DECISION: 
The Supreme Court dismissed the appeal, affirming the conviction and sentence imposed on the accused.

## COURT'S REASONING: 
The court found that the accused came to the house of the deceased with the intention of questioning them regarding the visit made to the deity installed in the disputed property. They also established that the accused carried deadly weapons, which clearly points to premeditation and intention to cause injuries likely to cause death.
